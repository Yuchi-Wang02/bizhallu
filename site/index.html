AI reliability for business analytics

Detecting unsupported facts in LLM-generated retail analysis.

BizHallu turns transaction data into deterministic business questions, asks Qwen3-0.6B to write short analysis answers, and evaluates whether detector signals can flag incorrect numbers, entities, rankings, and conclusions.

Project Blueprint Experiment Design
Current phase GitHub Pages ready

Gold set 100 deterministic questions

Research question

Can simple internal signals detect confident business hallucinations?

The key unit is not the full answer. It is the business fact span: a number, percentage, country, product, month, ranking, comparison, or unsupported conclusion that a business user might rely on.

Observed behavior

Qwen3-0.6B makes useful, labelable business errors.

In the smoke test, Qwen selected wrong countries, products, rankings, and month-over-month values while still producing fluent business prose. Sampling and greedy decoding showed similar failure patterns, so the issue is not just random sampling noise.
