Next extension

Do this after the portfolio package, not before it.

These are the strongest follow-up questions because they connect the hallucination detector to accounting and supply-management decisions.

  1. Which products have high gross sales but poor net revenue after returns, and should they be reviewed for quality or demand issues?
  2. Which monthly revenue changes are driven by returns rather than sales growth or decline?
  3. Which top-3 products are most vulnerable to rank changes if return activity increases?
  4. Which country contributes the largest non-UK revenue exposure after excluding one-off cancellations?
  5. Which product categories should be prioritized for replenishment because they appear in recurring top-product answers?
  6. Which answer claims should be verified against structured evidence before being sent to a stakeholder?
  7. Which detector false negatives correspond to the highest business risk because the hallucinated span is a currency amount or rank?
  8. Which detector false positives are acceptable as conservative review flags in accounting-style workflows?
  9. How often does Qwen copy a real amount but bind it to the wrong product or rank?
  10. Would an evidence-aware verifier catch errors that entropy and top-2 margin miss?
Implementation rule: keep Qwen3-0.6B and Online Retail as the core setup. Add a small 10-20 question business-risk extension before considering a larger benchmark or a new model family.
