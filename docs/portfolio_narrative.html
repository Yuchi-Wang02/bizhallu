Core Story

What this project is trying to prove.

The project is not a generic chatbot demo. It is a business reliability experiment: when an LLM writes a sales-analysis answer, can we identify which exact business facts are supported by the data and which are hallucinated or mis-bound?

  1. Business problem

    Analytics users can be harmed by a wrong revenue amount, wrong top product, wrong rank, or wrong comparison direction even when the prose sounds fluent.

  2. Controlled evidence

    UCI Online Retail transaction data is cleaned into deterministic gold answers, so every question can be checked against source rows.

  3. Model behavior

    Qwen3-0.6B often copies plausible evidence values but binds them to the wrong product, rank, country, or period.

  4. Detector question

    Internal uncertainty and energy-style signals provide useful signal, but they miss some confident business-context errors.

  5. Portfolio claim

    The strongest takeaway is not that the detector is finished. It is that business AI needs evidence-aware validation beyond token confidence.

Experimental design

  • Generated 100 deterministic questions across 7 business question types.
  • Ran Qwen/Qwen3-0.6B locally on cuda:0 with seed 42.
  • Annotated 205 held-out spans across 35 dev/test questions.
  • Aligned 205 spans to token traces with simple uncertainty and energy-ready score fields.
  • Selected thresholds on dev spans and reported metrics on held-out test spans.

Why span-level labels matter

  • Whole-answer labels are too coarse because one answer can mix correct and wrong facts.
  • Business users often rely on a single rank, amount, or comparison direction.
  • The label schema separates correct_key_fact, hallucinated_key_fact, and unsupported_claim.
  • The portfolio demo uses locked spans so the public examples are traceable to source rows.

Results

Internal signals help, but confident evidence-binding errors remain.

The detector result is credible because it is split-safe: thresholds are chosen on dev spans, then reused unchanged on held-out test spans. The strongest current signal comes from simple token uncertainty, not from a clean pure Spilled Energy win.

Best ranking signal

0.835 test AUPRC

one_minus_min_top2_margin ranks wrong spans best, with test precision 0.854 and recall 0.672 at the dev-selected threshold.

Best thresholded F1

0.779 test F1

mean_token_entropy increases recall to 0.869, but specificity falls to 0.476.

Energy nuance

0.773 best energy F1

The best energy-family row is mean_spilled_probability_mass_after_top2, a probability-mass control rather than pure adjacent-step Spilled Energy.

Observed failure patterns

  • Simple best-AUPRC baseline produced 20 false negatives and 7 false positives on held-out test error review rows.
  • Energy best-F1 baseline produced 10 false negatives and 20 false positives.
  • Currency amounts and top-3 product rows are the most important miss patterns.
  • Correct numeric and context spans can be over-flagged, so detector claims must remain qualified.

Interpretation

  • Simple uncertainty is the strongest current held-out signal: it leads both overall AUPRC and overall F1.
  • The best energy-family row is a probability-mass control, not pure adjacent-step Spilled Energy.
  • AUPRC and F1 tell different stories: the best-AUPRC detector is more precise, while the best-F1 detector raises recall at the cost of specificity.
  • Top-3 product questions remain the clearest confident-error failure mode.
  • Correct numeric and context facts are still over-flagged, so detector claims must stay qualified.

Personal Branding

How to present this as your project.

The strongest positioning is not "I made another AI demo." It is: I can translate business data problems into rigorous AI evaluation workflows.

One-minute pitch
  1. BizHallu is my AI reliability project for business analysis. I used UCI Online Retail data to generate deterministic retail analytics questions with known gold answers.
  2. I ran Qwen3-0.6B locally, captured token-level traces, and labeled exact business fact spans such as product names, ranks, countries, months, currency amounts, and percentages.
  3. The core finding is that the model often sounds analytical while binding evidence incorrectly. It may copy a real amount from the table, but assign it to the wrong rank or product.
  4. Simple internal uncertainty signals help, with held-out test AUPRC 0.835, but they still miss confident ranking and amount-binding errors. That is why business-context validation matters.
LinkedIn / portfolio blurb

I built BizHallu to study hallucinations in business analytics, not as abstract chatbot errors but as concrete mistakes in revenue, ranking, product, and evidence binding. The project turns retail transactions into deterministic questions, runs Qwen3-0.6B locally, labels exact business fact spans, and evaluates whether internal uncertainty signals catch unsupported claims.
